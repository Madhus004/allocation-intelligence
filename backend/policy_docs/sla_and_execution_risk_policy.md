# SLA and Execution Risk Policy

## Purpose
Protect delivery promise and execution confidence during allocation review.

## Policy Rules
- Do not recommend an option if estimated transit time puts promised delivery at risk, unless no other feasible option exists.
- Nodes with elevated weather disruption or very high capacity utilization should be treated as execution-risk nodes.
- If a lower-cost option creates meaningful SLA or labor-delay risk, the agent should favor the option with higher fulfillment confidence.

## Guidance
- A slightly higher shipping cost is acceptable if it materially improves SLA confidence.
- Favor options with stable replenishment, lower operational risk, and strong execution confidence.
